# Zomato Analysis
## Project-Objective
- To analyze Zomato restaurant data to uncover trends in customer preferences, pricing, and restaurant performance.
To clean, transform, and visualize the dataset for meaningful insights that support data-driven decision-making.
To create clear dashboards and analytical reports that help stakeholders understand key KPIs and business patterns.

## Process
- Imported and understood the Zomato dataset, identifying key fields like ratings, cuisines, cost, and location.
- Cleaned and prepared the data by removing duplicates, handling missing values, and standardizing formats.
- Performed exploratory data analysis (EDA) to identify trends in ratings, pricing, cuisines, and city-wise performance.
- Created visualizations and dashboards to represent key KPIs such as average rating, cost for two, and cuisine popularity.
- Derived insights and recommendations based on the analysis to support data-driven decision-making.

## Project Insights
- North Indian, Chinese, and Fast Food cuisines emerged as the most popular based on high ratings and large vote counts.
- Mid-range restaurants (₹300–₹600 cost for two) received the highest customer engagement, showing strong value-for-money preference.
- City-wise analysis revealed that metro cities have a higher concentration of high-rated restaurants, indicating stronger competition and quality standards.
- Ratings were strongly influenced by factors like cuisine variety, pricing, and service quality, while overly expensive restaurants showed inconsistent rating patterns.
- High-vote restaurants consistently maintained above-average ratings, suggesting customer trust and operational consistency.

## Conclusion
- The analysis highlights clear customer preferences in cuisines, pricing, and restaurant quality, helping identify what drives higher ratings and engagement.
- Insights show that mid-range pricing and popular cuisines deliver the most consistent performance across cities, indicating strong market demand.
- Overall, the project demonstrates how data-driven analysis can support restaurants and food-delivery platforms in making informed decisions to improve customer satisfaction and business growth.
